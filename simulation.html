How does this work? & sources

Each preset is a probability density that might plausibly describe a population of LoL player skills. The page numerically normalises each pdf, computes its cdf, and inverts it at every NA-2025 rank cutoff. Each distribution is then standardised to mean 0 and std 1 so shapes are directly comparable on the σ-axis.

Because rank cutoffs are at fixed cumulative percentiles, every distribution preserves the same population fraction in each rank — only the σ-width of skill each rank covers changes with the shape.

Distributions cited from the rating-systems / psychometrics / ranking literature:

  • Normal — TrueSkill (Herbrich et al., 2007), Glicko (Glickman, 1995): individual ratings are Gaussian.
  • Logistic — the implicit shape behind Bradley–Terry (1952) and Elo: pairwise win probability is logistic in rating gap.
  • Gumbel / extreme value — Plackett–Luce ranking models (Plackett, 1975) and the multinomial-logit family.
  • Lognormal — multiplicative-skill assumption; Limpert et al. 2001 catalogue lognormal in human-performance data.
  • Gamma / Weibull — right-skewed positive distributions used in psychometrics and skill-acquisition / learning-curve studies.
  • Student t — robust-statistics version of Normal; heavier tails to handle extreme outliers.
  • Skew-normal — Azzalini (1985) extension of the normal; common as a flexible right-skewed alternative.
  • Bimodal mixtures — documented in matchmaking-data analyses where casual and grinder subpopulations show as two peaks.
  • Truncated normal — bounded skill (mechanical ceiling) variant.
